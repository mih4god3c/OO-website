Early renaissance
Early renaissance

In Early Renaissance, our algorithm managed to cluster the artworks into three main clusters. One cluster is made out of painitngs of Madonna holding a child, second cluster is represented by portraits and the third cluster consits of painitngs of Crucification. Portraits are painted from the shoulders up, with subjects not facing straight to the observer. We can see that the subjects are depicted with more anatomical realism versus the earlier art. Most of the motives are connected to religion, which is also very standard for the period of Early renaissance.

Some famous artists that were active in this period are: Masaccio, Donatello, Fra Angelico, Sandro Botticelli, Giovani Bellini.

Some famous art works from this period are: Expulsion from the garden of Eden, The Holy Trinity, The Annuciation, Flagelation of Christ, Primavera.

Northern renaissance
1430
Northern renaissance

Similar to the Early renaissance, the Northern renaissance artits were focused on painting religious motives. That is especially seen in sitting/laying poses where the algorithm detected pictures of Madonna holding a child as the most accurate pose for this period. Religious motives are also most common in standing poses, which are dominated with paintings of Adam and Eve. Portraits on the other hand look very similar to the ones presented in Early renaissance.

Some famous artists that were active in this period are: Jan van Eyck, Hieronymus Bosch, Albrecht Durer, Matthias Grunewald, Lucas Cranach the Elder.

Some famous art works from this period are: Ghent Altarpiece, The Arnolfini Portrait, The Descent from the Cross, Self-Portrait, Adam and Eve.

1490
High renaissance
High renaissance

Classification of High renaissance paintings, looks very similar to both other renaissance periods. We can see that there are again the same three clusters as before. There is a slight difference in portraits, where we noticed that more subjects are faicing straight to the observer. High renaissance is a period dominated with religious motives. There are still some differences between the High and the Early renaissance. Human figures are painted much more divine, and there is also more use of the linear perspective and shading.

Some famous artists that were active in this period are: Leonardo da Vinci, Michelangelo, Raphael, Andrea del Sarto, Correggio.

Some famous art works from this period are: Virgin of the rocks, The Last Supper, Mona Lisa, The Alba Madonna, School of Athens, The creation of Adam, The Sistine Madonna.

1600
Baroque
Baroque

For the period of Baroque, our algorithm managed to cluster the painitngs into the next three clusters. Poses where the subject is in laying position, portraits and painitngs containing standing subjects. We can see less religious motives and more emphasis on the human body. Paintings are also much more emphasizing beauty and majesty. In laying poses there are a lot of paintings representing a naked woman. We can also notice that portraits are drawn from further away. As for the standing poses, we can see that our algorithm still classified the painitng of Crusification as the best fit to the calculated median pose.

Some famous artists that were active in this period are: Caravaggio, Peter Paul Rubens, Artemisia Gentileschi, Gian Lorenzo Bernini, Rembrandt van Rijn.

Some famous art works from this period are: The calling of St Matthew, Judith and Holofernes, The night watch, Las Meninas.

Rococo
1723
Rococo

The period of Rococo is charaterized yb elaborate ornamentation, asymmetrical values and curves or serpentine lines. Paintings often depict themes of love, classical myths and playfulness. That is especially seen on vertical/standing poses. Paintings contains many more subjects and they are in a lot of more movement compared to earlier periods. Even the laying poses have more than one subjects, there is also a motive of a nude woman, similar to what we have seen in Baroque.

Some famous artists that were active in this period are: Francois boucher, Jean-Antoine Watteau, Jean-Honore Fragonard, Tiepolo.

Some famous art works from this period are: The embarkation for Cythera, Pierrot, Soap Bubbles, La Toilette de Venus, The swing.

Romanticism

The algorithm managed to cluster paintings from Romanticism into all three main clusters. We can notice some main charasteristics of Romanticism in laying poses which are dynamic, emotional and restless. In standing poses we can notice some individualism. The skeletons of portraits were too similar to portraits from other periods, so we could not determine any specifics.

Realism
1850
Realism

Artists in the period of Realism used their paintings to sarcastically depict political issues and problems. Others incorporated realism by painting scenes of the working class, rural and urban life. Painigns include as much detail as possible. shadows, life reflecting, depth perception and perspecitve are all done with perfection. We can see that on the images that our algorithm chose, that the subjects in standing poses really are representing the urban life. There is a lot more of movement and diverse poses. We can also notice that the algorithm did not find any horizontal poses, but it did manage to cluster together some portraits.

Some famous artists that were active in this period are: Gustave Courbet, Jean-Francois Millet, Edouard Manet, James Whistler, John Singer Sargent.

Some famous art works from this period are: Rue Transnonian, A burial at Ornans, The stone breakers, Symphony in white no.1, Olympia.

Post impressionism

Some famous artists that were active in this period are: Paul cezanne, Vincent van Gogh, Paul Gauguin, Georges Seurat.

Some famous art works from this period are: Sunday afternoon on the island of La Grande Jatte, Vision after the Sermon, Portrait of Doctor Gachet, The scream, The dream.

Pop art
1960
Pop art

As we can see, the algorithm managed to detect poses only in portraits. All other pictures were simply to abstract for our method to detect them, or they didn't even have any subjects, which is completely normal for the period of Pop art. Pop art utilized images and icons from popular media and products. This included commercial items like cans, road signs, newspapers etc. It also included pictures of celebrities, which we can clearly see in above collage. One problem, that lead to weak detections for this period was also that Pop art is characterized by bright colors, which made it even harder for our algorithm to detect humans.

Some famous artists that were active in this period are: Andy Warhol, Roy Lichtenstein, James Rosenquist, Claes Oldenburh, Eduardo Paolozzi.

Some famous art works from this period are: President elect, Drowning girl, Campbell's soup, A bigger splash.
